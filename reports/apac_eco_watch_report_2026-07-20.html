Executive summary
The APAC region (ex-Mainland China) presents a mixed but broadly stable macroeconomic picture for TLD Group. Manufacturing PMIs are stable across the region, with India and Vietnam in expansion, while Japan and South Korea hover near the 50 threshold. The China+1 trend continues to drive favorable FDI inflows into Southeast Asia and India, creating new industrial hubs and potential GSE demand. However, the persistent weakening of most APAC currencies (JPY, KRW, THB, PHP, IDR) against the USD is an unfavorable trend, raising import costs for TLD's USD-denominated equipment and parts and negatively impacting the translation of regional results into EUR. Trade integration through RCEP and CPTPP is improving, but the threat of new US tariffs on Southeast Asian exports introduces significant downside risk. Central bank policies remain stable, providing predictable financing conditions. The net impact on TLD APAC operations is a cautious outlook, with currency headwinds and tariff risks requiring active management. Priority actions include reviewing FX hedging strategies, mapping tariff savings under trade agreements, and preparing contingency plans for potential US tariffs.

APAC currencies weaken against USD, raising import costs

Reading
Most APAC currencies (JPY, KRW, THB, PHP, IDR) have weakened against the USD over the past 6-12 months due to persistent US dollar strength and divergent monetary policies. This trend is unfavorable for TLD as it raises import costs for USD-denominated equipment purchases and parts.
