Executive summary
The Asia-Pacific region (ex-Mainland China) presents a mixed but broadly positive economic picture for TLD Group in mid-2026. Manufacturing PMIs across South Korea (54.8), India (54.7), Taiwan (55.2), and Thailand (52.6) remain firmly in expansion territory, signaling robust industrial health and capex appetite. However, the Middle East conflict has triggered significant currency depreciation across the region, with the Indian rupee falling 5.2% against the USD since late February, breaching the critical 3% threshold and requiring immediate FX hedging attention. Central banks are responding with hawkish stances — Bank Indonesia raised rates 50bps to 5.50%, the Bank of Korea signals imminent tightening, and the RBA holds at 4.35% with further hikes possible — increasing financing costs for TLD's regional entities and customers. FDI inflows remain strong, with India up 18% YoY to US$58.85 billion and Thailand up 73% YoY, reinforcing the "China+1" trend and creating new customer opportunities. The net impact on TLD APAC operations is a favorable demand environment offset by rising financing costs and FX volatility, requiring proactive hedging and debt management.
🌏 Regional competitiveness outlook (30-90 days)
The regional cost base is becoming more competitive for manufacturing hubs like India, Vietnam, and Thailand due to currency depreciation, which benefits TLD's sourcing operations. However, rising local interest rates and imported inflation from the Middle East conflict may erode some of these gains over the next 30-90 days.
🏦 Financing / credit outlook (90-180 days)
Regional financing conditions are tightening, with South Korea, Indonesia, and Australia all signaling or implementing rate hikes. TLD's regional entities should expect higher local currency borrowing costs over the next 90-180 days, while the strong USD environment will continue to pressure Asian currencies and increase FX translation risk.
