India remains a powerhouse with a PMI of 56.9 in February, though growth is moderating to 54.2 by June, and record FDI inflows are tempered by low net investment and portfolio outflows. Vietnam is a standout, with record FDI disbursement of $13 billion in H1 and a PMI consistently above 50, cementing its role as a key "China+1" destination. However, Indonesia faces significant headwinds: a surprise 25 bps rate hike to 5.50% to combat a sinking rupiah and record-high cost burdens in manufacturing. South Korea's PMI is expanding but faces surging input cost inflation and the prospect of rate hikes. Thailand and the Philippines show tentative recovery, while Taiwan's industrial sector is very strong. The net impact on TLD APAC is a favorable demand environment in Japan, India, Vietnam, and Taiwan, offset by rising financing costs and FX risks in Indonesia and South Korea. Priority actions include hedging FX exposure in Indonesia and South Korea, expanding the Vietnam footprint, and leveraging Thailand's low interest rates for customer financing.
COMPETITIVENESS_OUTLOOK: The regional cost base is under upward pressure from rising input costs in Indonesia and South Korea, and a weaker yen and won are making Japanese and Korean exports more competitive but hurting TLD's EUR-denominated reporting. Vietnam and India remain the most competitive manufacturing destinations due to strong FDI inflows and stable currencies, making them attractive for TLD's supply chain expansion over the next 30-90 days.
FINANCING_OUTLOOK: Financing conditions are diverging sharply across the region. Indonesia's surprise rate hike to 5.50% signals a tightening bias, while Thailand's 1% rate offers a cheap financing haven. South Korea is on the cusp of rate hikes, while India and Vietnam are expected to remain stable. TLD should prioritize locking in low-cost financing in Thailand and prepare for higher costs in Indonesia and South Korea over the next 90-180 days.
WATCH_1: Indonesia PMI and rupiah: If the PMI falls below 50 or the rupiah breaches IDR 17,000/USD, expect further rate hikes and increased FX risk.
WATCH_2: Bank of Korea rate decision: A 25 bps hike at the next meeting would signal the start of a tightening cycle, impacting TLD Korea's financing costs.
WATCH_3: India net FDI and FPI flows: A continued decline in net foreign investment could trigger a sharp rupee depreciation and increase country risk.
MAIN_RISK: A sustained period of currency weakness and rising interest rates in Indonesia and South Korea, driven by global inflation and US rate differentials, could significantly increase TLD's financing costs and FX translation losses in two of its key APAC markets.
MAIN_OPPORTUNITY: Vietnam's record FDI inflows and strong manufacturing PMI present a clear opportunity for TLD to expand its supply chain and assembly operations in the country, capitalizing on the "China+1" trend and a stable currency environment.
