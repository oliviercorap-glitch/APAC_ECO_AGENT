Pos Indonesia default tests state rescue fund Danantara

Reading
Pos Indonesia, a state-owned enterprise, has defaulted on its obligations, testing the effectiveness of the Danantara rescue strategy. This event raises concerns about the financial health of Indonesian state-owned enterprises and the government's capacity to manage fiscal risks.
Supply chain impact
No direct supply chain impact. TLD's manufacturing and sourcing operations in Indonesia are not directly affected by this specific SOE default.
Financing / FX impact
The default could increase Indonesia's sovereign risk premium, potentially leading to higher borrowing costs for all entities operating in the country, including TLD's regional entity. It may also put downward pressure on the Indonesian rupiah (IDR) against the EUR and USD, affecting translation of local entity results and the cost of IDR-denominated financing for customers.
Recommended action
Monitor IDR exchange rate and Indonesian sovereign CDS spreads for any widening. Review TLD Indonesia entity's local currency financing arrangements and assess if any customer financing deals are exposed to SOE-linked credit risk. No immediate action required this week.
