Important 📈 GDP Taiwan

Taiwan GDP growth forecast raised to 10.35%, exports to top USD 900 billion.

Reading
The Chung-Hua Institution for Economic Research (CIER) has lifted Taiwan's 2026 GDP growth forecast to 10.35%, driven by record export performance. Exports are projected to surpass USD 900 billion this year, reflecting strong global demand for semiconductors and advanced electronics.
Supply chain impact
Taiwan's booming export economy signals robust industrial activity and high capacity utilization among key electronics and semiconductor suppliers. This could tighten supply chains for components used in TLD's GSE, potentially leading to longer lead times or higher input costs for electronics and control systems sourced from or through Taiwan.
Financing / FX impact
Strong GDP and export growth are likely to support the New Taiwan Dollar (TWD) against the USD and EUR. A stronger TWD would increase the cost base for any TLD entity sourcing from Taiwan, but also improve the translation of any Taiwan-based entity's earnings into EUR. The robust economy may also lead to tighter local monetary conditions.
Recommended action
For CFO APAC: Monitor TWD exchange rate trends and assess any impact on component sourcing costs from Taiwan. Review lead times for any Taiwan-sourced electronics with the supply chain team this week.

Reading

Supply chain impact
New US tariffs will directly impact any TLD equipment or components exported from APAC (ex-China) facilities to the US market. Important 🌐 TRADE Regional (APAC ex-China)

US pivots to durable Section 301 tariffs, escalating trade friction.

Reading
The US is expected to pivot to more durable tariffs under Section 301, moving beyond temporary measures. Analysts expect this to create a more managed but persistent state of trade friction between the US and China, with significant spillover effects for the broader APAC supply chain.
Supply chain impact
A shift to durable Section 301 tariffs will accelerate "China+1" supply chain diversification, benefiting countries like Vietnam, India, and Thailand as manufacturing hubs. For TLD, this could create new customer bases in these markets but also increase supply chain complexity and cost as regional sourcing networks are reconfigured.
Financing / FX impact
Persistent trade friction will keep APAC currencies under pressure against the USD, particularly for trade-dependent economies. This creates a sustained headwind for TLD's regional entity results when translated to EUR. It also raises the risk of capital flow volatility in emerging APAC markets.
Recommended action
CFO APAC: Task the regional treasury team to model FX scenarios for a sustained USD strength environment. Engage with commercial teams to identify potential new customer opportunities in "China+1" beneficiary markets.

China-led digital currency platform mBridge processes record USD 1.7 billion transfers.

Reading
Bank of China used the mBridge platform for two cross-border transfers worth over USD 1.7 billion, the largest transactions to date on the central bank digital currency (CBDC) platform. mBridge involves the central banks of China, Hong Kong, Thailand, and the UAE.
Supply chain impact
No direct supply chain impact. The development of CBDC platforms like mBridge could eventually streamline cross-border payments for trade settlement, potentially reducing transaction costs and settlement times for regional supply chains, but this is a long-term evolution.
Financing / FX impact
The growth of mBridge signals a potential shift away from the USD-dominated SWIFT system for regional trade settlement. For TLD, this could offer an alternative, potentially lower-cost channel for cross-border payments between entities and suppliers in participating jurisdictions (e.g., Hong Kong, Thailand). It also introduces a new regulatory and compliance dimension for treasury operations.
Recommended action
CFO APAC: Instruct the regional treasury team to monitor mBridge developments and assess the platform's potential for future intercompany or supplier payments in Hong Kong and Thailand. No immediate action required.
