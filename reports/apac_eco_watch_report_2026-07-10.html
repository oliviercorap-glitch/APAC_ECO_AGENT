Executive summary
The APAC region (ex-China) presents a highly divergent economic picture for TLD Group. Japan is the dominant story, with the yen at a 40-year low against the USD, creating significant FX translation risk for TLD Japan but also making Japanese suppliers highly competitive. Bank Indonesia's aggressive 50 bps rate hike to 5.25% is a critical event, sharply increasing financing costs in Indonesia. In contrast, the Bank of Thailand is cutting rates, offering a more favorable financing environment. The Philippines is a bright spot, with a 52% surge in Q1 FDI signaling strong industrial growth and a potential GSE demand tailwind. Vietnam's manufacturing PMI remains expansionary but is slowing, while Malaysia's PMI shows renewed easing. The "China Plus One" trend and the RCEP trade agreement continue to shape the regional supply chain landscape, favoring Vietnam and other ASEAN economies. The primary risk is the volatile yen and its impact on TLD Japan's financials, while the main opportunity is the surging FDI in the Philippines.
🌏 Regional competitiveness outlook (30-90 days)
The net regional competitiveness outlook for the next 30-90 days is mixed. A very weak yen makes Japan a highly competitive sourcing and manufacturing base, potentially pressuring TLD's own production in other regions. However, rising financing costs in Indonesia and a slowing PMI in Malaysia are headwinds. The Philippines and Vietnam remain attractive for "China+1" related investment, supporting their industrial ecosystems.
🏦 Financing / credit outlook (90-180 days)
The regional financing outlook for the next 90-180 days is highly divergent. Financing costs are rising sharply in Indonesia following the 50 bps rate hike, while they are falling in Thailand. Japan faces potential rate hikes from the BOJ, which would increase local financing costs. The Philippines may see improved credit conditions due to strong FDI inflows. TLD must manage a complex, multi-speed financing environment across its APAC entities.
